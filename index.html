The Problem

Office Markets Are Competing
on Price. That Is a Dead End.

Downtown office vacancy is at historic highs. Remote work has reshaped demand. Landlords are cutting rents, offering concessions, and relying on brokers to fill space. The result: buildings are treated as generic, interchangeable square footage.

The Opportunity

These Buildings Sit in Canada's
Federal Policy Corridor

Promenade du Portage is not a generic office address. It sits at the heart of a concentrated zone of federal decision-making — steps from Parliament Hill, Place du Portage, and the Chaudière–Zibi district. This proximity is a strategic asset that most landlords are failing to monetize.

Organizations that work with or around government — GovTech, consulting, public affairs, venture capital, policy research — need to be here. They just need a reason to choose these buildings.

How It Works

Three Engines Drive
Tenant Demand

The strategy creates demand through three mutually reinforcing engines. Together, they generate the gravity that draws tenants organically — beyond what brokers alone can deliver.

I

Workspace

Private offices, coworking desks, bookable meeting rooms, and event spaces. Flexible formats for organizations of every size.

II

Ecosystem

Targeted tenant clustering — organizations that benefit from proximity to each other and to government. A curated community, not a random tenant mix.

III

Visibility

Professional events, public conversations, and media. The campus becomes known as the place where government and innovation intersect.
