## Post 1: Crisis Leadership Authority

Most people talk about crisis leadership from textbooks.

I lived it from inside the machine.

During the 2008 global financial crisis, I served in Canada's Prime Minister's Office on the Quebec Desk, managing issues at the intersection of politics, economics, and public confidence. The decisions made in those rooms affected millions of Canadians — and they had to be made fast, with incomplete information, under enormous political pressure.

A decade later, as Chief of Staff at the Senate of Canada during the COVID-19 pandemic, the playbook had to be rewritten again. Different crisis, same demand: clarity under pressure, decisive action, and the ability to hold an institution together when everything around it is shifting.

Here's what I learned that no leadership book will teach you:

The quality of a decision made at the right time beats a perfect decision made too late. Every time.

That principle — speed, clarity, execution — is the foundation of everything I bring to the entrepreneurs and business leaders I advise today through my firm, IBPROM Corp.

If you've built something significant and you're navigating a moment that feels like it could define the next decade of your business, that's the conversation I want to have.

## Post 2: SPARK Framework Introduction

Every organization I've worked with — from the Prime Minister's Office to the Senate of Canada to private enterprises — has the same core challenge:

They know WHAT needs to happen. They struggle with HOW to make it happen fast enough.

That's why I built the SPARK Framework:

S — Strategic Alignment: Every decision, resource, and action oriented toward one coherent direction.
P — People & Talent Optimization: Right people, right roles, cultures that perform under pressure.
A — Adaptive Innovation & AI: Technology and adaptation as operational defaults, not afterthoughts.
R — Relationship & Communication: Trust and influence as core business assets.
K — Key Decision-Making & Execution: High-stakes decisions with clarity, then execution without hesitation.

SPARK isn't theoretical. It's built from decisions made during a global financial crisis and a pandemic, refined through advisory work with entrepreneurs and business leaders who don't have time for frameworks that look good on slides but collapse under real pressure.

If your organization has the talent but not the traction, the problem is almost always in one of these five areas.

## Post 5: AI and Advisory — The Future of Strategic Counsel

The advisory profession is being split in two:

Advisors who use AI as a force multiplier — and advisors who will be replaced by it.

I run an AI-first advisory practice. Every layer of IBPROM Corp — from research and analysis to client communication to strategic scenario planning — is augmented by artificial intelligence. Not because it's trendy, but because a solo operator competing with firms 50x his size has no choice but to multiply every hour by ten.

Here's what AI can't replace: the judgment that comes from serving in the Prime Minister's Office during a global financial crisis. The instinct built through leading Senate operations during a pandemic. The trust that only forms when a client looks across the table and sees someone who has been tested under the same kind of pressure they face.

AI handles the volume. Human judgment handles the stakes.

That's the "A" in my SPARK Framework — Adaptive Innovation & AI. Not AI for its own sake, but AI deployed as a competitive weapon inside every advisory engagement.
