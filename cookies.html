What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites function, remember your preferences, and understand how users interact with the site. Cookies can be either "session cookies" (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent cookies" (stored for a longer period).

Capital Corridor Campus respects your privacy. This policy explains what cookies we use, why we use them, and how you can control them.

What Cookies Does Capital Corridor Campus Use?

Capital Corridor Campus is a static website hosted on GitHub Pages. We do not operate login systems, e-commerce functionality, or user accounts. Our cookie footprint is minimal:

Essential Cookies

We do not set essential cookies ourselves. However, GitHub Pages (our hosting provider) may set minimal technical cookies required for site security and performance. These are necessary for the site to function properly.

No Tracking Cookies

Capital Corridor Campus does not use advertising cookies, analytics cookies, or behavioral tracking. We do not build user profiles or track your activity across the web.

Third-Party Cookies and External Services

Our site uses the following external services, which may set cookies on your device:

  • Google Fonts: We import fonts from Google Fonts for typography. Google Fonts may store a cookie to remember your font preferences. Google Privacy Policy

Note: Capital Corridor Campus does not currently use Google Analytics or other third-party analytics services. If we add analytics in the future, we will update this policy and obtain your consent in compliance with Quebec Law 25.

How to Manage Cookies

You can control and manage cookies through your web browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View what cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete cookies individually or in bulk
  • Block cookies from specific websites
  • Enable "Do Not Track" signals (though not all sites honor these)

Browser Instructions:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Note: Disabling all cookies may affect your ability to view certain website features. Essential cookies are required for the site to work properly.

Quebec Law 25 Compliance

Capital Corridor Campus complies with Law 25 (Quebec's updated privacy law), which strengthens data protection rights and requires transparency about how personal information is collected and used.

Key principles guiding our cookie practice:

  • Transparency: We clearly disclose what cookies we use and why.
  • Minimal Collection: We collect only the data necessary to operate the website.
  • Your Control: You can manage cookies directly through your browser.
  • No Tracking: We do not engage in behavioral or cross-site tracking.
  • No Sale: We do not sell or share your data with third parties.

If Capital Corridor Campus adds services in the future that require consent (such as analytics), we will update this policy and obtain your explicit consent before implementing them.
