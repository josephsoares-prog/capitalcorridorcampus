A New Chapter for 179 Promenade du Portage

Street-level commercial space meets education vision. An exclusive interview with Joseph Soares on the ground floor opportunity and the vision for a hybrid salon-academy partnership.

Interviewer
Campus Corridor Insights
Interviewee
Joseph Soares, MBA, PMP, Adm.A. — Administrator, Capital Corridor Campus

The Opportunity At A Glance

Location Ground Floor, 179 Promenade du Portage
Space Size ~1,200 sq ft
Lease Rate $4,800/month (all-inclusive)
Building Certification LEED Gold
Condition Turnkey ready — salon equipment installed
Q: Joseph, congratulations on opening up the ground floor. Can you start by describing what's available?

Thank you. Yes, we're excited to announce that our ground floor commercial space at 179 Promenade du Portage is now available for lease. We're looking at approximately 1,200 square feet of street-level space that has exceptional visibility and foot traffic. The real win here is that it comes fully turnkey from a previous high-end salon operation. The space already has wash stations installed, professional styling chairs, mirrors, and all the infrastructure that a beauty or wellness operation would need to open immediately. All of that is in place. The monthly rate is $4,800, and that includes everything—utilities, common area maintenance, building services, the works. All-inclusive.

Q: That's a unique setup—a salon-ready space in the capital district. What's your vision for how this space could be used?

This is where it gets interesting. We're not just looking to fill the space with another salon operation, though that would certainly work. Our vision is to create something hybrid—a working salon combined with a professional training academy. We want to partner with an education provider who understands that model. Think of it this way: during the day, the space operates as a functioning, revenue-generating salon serving clients in the government district and surrounding area. Evenings or designated hours, it becomes a classroom and training facility where students learn professional salon and beauty services in a real working environment.

The hybrid salon-academy model creates a dual revenue stream while building a talent pipeline for the beauty and wellness industry in the National Capital Region.

Q: That's compelling. Why do you think that model makes sense for this specific location?

Location is everything, and 179 Promenade du Portage is one of the most strategically positioned addresses in Canada. We're steps from Place du Portage, which sits at the intersection of federal employment hubs. Over 10,000 federal employees work within immediate walking distance of this building. Many of them are looking for convenience—lunch, professional services, wellness—all within a few minutes of their office. A high-end salon in this location would have built-in demand immediately.

But beyond that, we're in the heart of the bilingual corridor. Both Ottawa and Gatineau are served by this address. That bilingual advantage opens doors for recruitment, partnerships, and client base that extend beyond what a single-market salon could achieve. And the talent piece: if you're operating a salon-academy, you're not just serving current clients, you're developing the next generation of beauty and wellness professionals in a region where that talent is in demand.

Q: You mentioned you're looking for an education partner. What would you ideally like to see in that partnership?

We're actively seeking an education provider—whether that's an existing vocational school, a beauty institute, a professional development organization, or an entrepreneurial education group—who understands the salon and wellness industry and wants to build something in the capital region. The ideal partner would bring curriculum expertise, student recruitment capability, and a clear vision for how to operate a hybrid space.

The economics work for everyone in this model. The education partner gets a fully equipped facility without the capital burden of building out salon infrastructure. They have a real salon operation generating revenue that helps offset facility costs. And they're providing students with genuine, authentic learning experience in a working salon environment—which is exactly what employers want to see.

For the salon side, you have a built-in workforce pipeline. Your students become your junior stylists, eventually moving into paid positions as they progress. You're not constantly recruiting and training from scratch. It's a sustainable model.

Q: What does the building itself bring to this opportunity beyond the physical space?

This matters more than people realize. 179 Promenade du Portage is LEED Gold certified. That's not a marketing badge—it's a statement about building standards, air quality, energy efficiency, and workspace quality. For an education partner, it signals institutional credibility. You're operating in a facility that meets the same sustainability and operational standards that government and institutional clients expect. That's a competitive advantage when you're recruiting students or partnering with employers.

Beyond certification, the location itself is unmatched. We have immediate access to STO Rapibus and OC Transpo LRT. Public transit is a first-class consideration when you're recruiting students or attracting clients. You're not hidden in a strip mall on the edge of town. You're integrated into the center of power in the nation's capital. That accessibility, that visibility, that institutional positioning—it changes the entire value proposition.

The combination of turnkey salon infrastructure, LEED Gold certification, strategic location, and institutional credibility creates an exceptional platform for a salon-academy partnership.

Q: For someone interested in this opportunity—whether as a salon operator or as an education partner—how should they proceed?

We're actively looking to move on this. The space is available now, and we're ready to engage with serious partners. The first step is to reach out directly. Contact us at info@campuscorridor.ca or call 873-455-3667. Let's have a conversation about what you're envisioning. Are you a salon operator interested in the space as-is? Are you an education provider interested in the hybrid model? Are you someone who wants to build something new in the capital? Let's talk about what fits.

We can arrange a site visit, walk through the space, discuss the building infrastructure, talk about what's possible. There's real potential here, and we want to work with people who see it.

Q: What does this space represent for Capital Corridor Campus as a whole?

It represents our evolution. We've built a reputation as a hub for government-adjacent professionals—consultants, advisors, lobbyists, policy entrepreneurs. That continues to be core to what we do. But we're also expanding the vision of what a mixed-use professional campus can be. We want to host work that matters, talent development that serves the region, and partnerships that create real economic activity.

The ground floor salon-academy is exactly that. It's not just commercial lease space. It's an ecosystem. Clients coming in for salon services encounter a professional building environment. Students training in the space are exposed to institutional standards and professional culture from day one. Education and commerce interact. That's where real value is created.

This is the next chapter for 179 Promenade du Portage. We're excited about what comes next.
