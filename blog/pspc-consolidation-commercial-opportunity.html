How PSPC Consolidation Created Opportunity in the Government Corridor

Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC) manages approximately 6 million square meters of office space across the country. Since 2020, PSPC has been systematically consolidating its footprint, closing satellite offices, and shifting government work to hybrid arrangements.

This consolidation freed up enormous amounts of commercial space. Downtown Gatineau had vacancy rates climbing. Landlords who'd counted on stable long-term government contracts faced a choice: reconfigure their buildings for a new market, or accept declining occupancy.

The Market Impact

For private-sector landlords and innovators, this created an opening. The space was there. The opportunity was there. But the tenant profile needed to shift.

As government reduces direct footprint, private-sector professionals need independent addresses. Capital Corridor Campus fills this gap with flexible, affordable office solutions.

The Opportunity

This shift is exactly why flexible office models work in the government corridor. The Corridor Address ($99/month) serves government-adjacent professionals who need credibility without overhead. Facilities that are LEED Gold certified, steps from Place du Portage, with direct STO Rapibus access—position tenants at the center of the government ecosystem.
