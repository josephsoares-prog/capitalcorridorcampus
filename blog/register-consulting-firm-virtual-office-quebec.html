How to Register Your Consulting Firm with a Virtual Office Address in Quebec

Step-by-step guide to legal registration using a virtual office address.

If you're starting a consulting practice in Quebec, you need a business address. Federal incorporation requires it. NEQ registration requires it. CRA requires it. But that address doesn't have to be a $3,000-per-month office lease. A virtual office address works for all legal and regulatory purposes—and it costs $99/month.

Here's how to register your consulting firm in Quebec using a virtual office address, and why it's the right move for a new solo practice.

Understanding Quebec Business Registration Requirements

Quebec requires business owners to register in two places: with the Registrar of Enterprises (Registraire des entreprises) at the provincial level, and potentially with Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada (ISED) if you're federally incorporated.

Both require a business address. That address must be a physical location in Quebec (or Canada, if federally incorporated). It can be residential, commercial, or a virtual office. The government doesn't care which—they just need a verifiable address where you receive official correspondence.

A virtual office address satisfies this requirement completely. You're not breaking any rules. You're just using the most cost-effective option available.

The Three Registration Paths

Option 1: Sole Proprietor (Unregistered)
If you operate as a sole proprietor under your own name with no employees, you can register informally with CRA for a business number. You still need an address, but you can use a home address. However, a business address signals professionalism.

Option 2: NEQ Registration (Quebec Sole Proprietorship)
If you want a registered business name in Quebec, you file with the Registraire des entreprises (REQ). This requires a business address. Processing takes 2-3 business days. Cost: $18. You receive a NEQ (Numéro d'Enregistrement d'Entreprise du Quebec). A virtual office address works perfectly here.

Option 3: Federal Incorporation (Corp. or Inc.)
If you want to incorporate federally, you file with Corporations Canada. This requires an office address in Canada. A virtual office address in Gatineau or Ottawa counts. Cost: $275. Processing takes 2-3 business days. You receive a Federal Business Number and corporate status.

Why a Virtual Office Works (Legally)

The Registraire des entreprises doesn't require you to personally occupy the office. They just require a verifiable address where official mail can be received. A virtual office at Capital Corridor Campus provides exactly that: professional mail handling, verified receipt, and forwarding to your actual location.

CRA similarly doesn't verify occupancy. They mail your business number and statements to your registered address. A virtual office handles this seamlessly.

A virtual office address is legally equivalent to a physical office for registration purposes. The government cares that you have a verifiable business address, not that you personally sit at a desk there.

Step-by-Step Registration Process

Sign up for a Corridor Address (or higher tier) at Capital Corridor Campus. You receive confirmation, your business address, and mail handling details within 24 hours.

Step 2: File Your NEQ (Quebec) or Federal Incorporation
Visit the Registraire des entreprises website (registreentreprises.gouv.qc.ca) for NEQ registration, or Corporations Canada (ic.gc.ca) for federal incorporation. You'll need your virtual office address and contact information. File online. Pay the fee ($18 for NEQ, $275 for federal). Processing takes 2-3 business days.

Step 3: Obtain Your Business Number from CRA
Once you're registered provincially or federally, apply for a CRA Business Number at businessnumberregistration.gc.ca. You'll provide your NEQ or federal incorporation number, your business address (your virtual office), and contact information. CRA mails your business number to your registered address within 5-10 business days.

Step 4: Register for GST/HST and QST (If Required)
If your annual revenue will exceed $30,000, you must register for GST/HST (federal) and QST (Quebec). You do this through CRA. Your virtual office address is your registered address for tax correspondence.
